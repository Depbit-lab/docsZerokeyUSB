---
title: "Browser extension"
description: "Install the ZeroKeyUSB Web Link and fill logins with one click."
---

The optional **ZeroKeyUSB Web Link** extension for Chrome/Edge speeds up logins:
click the toolbar icon on a login page and the device jumps to the matching
site and focuses the login field — you just pick the site on the device and it
types the credential.

<Note>
The extension never sees your password. It only tells the device which letter to
look for and focuses a field; the credential is typed by the device after you
physically pick it. How it works under the hood: [Browser link](/firmware/browser-link).
</Note>

## Install

<Steps>
<Step title="Load the extension">
Open `chrome://extensions`, turn on **Developer mode**, choose **Load unpacked**
and select the `chrome-extension/` folder.
</Step>
<Step title="Link your device (once)">
Click the toolbar icon → **Connect ZeroKeyUSB…**, then pick the device's port on
the page that opens. It is remembered afterwards.
</Step>
</Steps>

The toolbar icon shows a **green dot** when the device is linked and a **grey
dot** when it isn't.

## Using it

<Steps>
<Step title="Open the site">
Go to a website where you have an account, and make sure the ZeroKeyUSB is
plugged in and **unlocked**.
</Step>
<Step title="Click the icon">
The device jumps to the site's first letter and the page's login field gets
focused.
</Step>
<Step title="Pick and type">
Choose the matching site on the device and press — it types your username and
password.
</Step>
</Steps>

## Turning it off

Don't want it? On the device, set **Menu → Tools → `Chrome: Off`** and it will
ignore the extension entirely. It ships **On**.

<Note>
It works on most login pages. Sites that split the username and password across
separate screens (some Google/Microsoft flows) may not fill correctly.
</Note>
